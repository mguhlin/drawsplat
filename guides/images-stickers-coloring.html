Feature Guide

Images, Stickers, and Coloring

Add visual prompts, classroom stickers, coloring pages, animal images, and student-created pictures to make the board more concrete and engaging.

What this covers

  • Load Image: place an uploaded image, PDF page, or supported slide image onto the board.
  • Image Gallery: choose built-in classroom images and coloring assets.
  • Stickers: place quick visual labels, feedback markers, or icons.
  • Crop and masks: shape images into circles, triangles, stars, hearts, and other classroom-friendly forms.
  • Coloring Book: insert line-art pages for annotation, coloring, labeling, or download.

Classroom ideas

  • Insert an image and ask students to label parts with arrows and sticky notes.
  • Use stickers as quick status markers: checked, question, idea, pin, book, or search.
  • Place an animal photo and build a picture graph, habitat map, or observation board.
  • Insert a coloring page and ask students to color-code evidence, vocabulary, or categories.
  • Crop a picture into a shape to create cleaner activity cards.

Suggested workflow

  1. Choose Insert, then load an image or open the image gallery.
  2. Resize and position the image on the board.
  3. Use the inspector to crop, mask, duplicate, or adjust opacity.
  4. Add labels, arrows, stickers, or student response spaces around it.
  5. Lock the image as a background when students should work on top of it without moving it.

Tips

  • Use lower-resolution images when students are on shared devices or slow connections.
  • Keep labels separate from the image if students need to move answers around.
  • Use the built-in gallery when you want local assets that do not depend on external websites.
